PCOD Frequently Asked Questions

Learn more about PCOD, stress, lifestyle care, and how OvaCare helps you track your wellness journey.

What causes PCOD? +
PCOD is commonly linked to hormonal imbalance, insulin resistance, genetics, and lifestyle factors such as poor food habits, lack of physical activity, and long-term stress.
Is PCOD curable? +
PCOD may not always be completely cured, but it can often be managed effectively with healthy lifestyle changes, regular exercise, diet control, stress management, and medical support when needed.
Can PCOD affect fertility? +
Yes, PCOD can affect ovulation and may make conception more difficult for some women. Many people still conceive successfully with proper treatment and lifestyle management.
How is PCOD diagnosed? +
Doctors usually diagnose PCOD using symptom history, menstrual pattern, blood tests, ultrasound scans, and clinical evaluation. A website tool alone cannot give a medical diagnosis.
Can exercise help manage PCOD? +
Yes. Regular exercise such as walking, yoga, cycling, stretching, and strength training can support weight management, insulin balance, mood, and hormone regulation.
Why does OvaCare include a stress quiz? +
Stress can affect sleep, appetite, energy, hormone balance, and daily habits. OvaCare tracks stress weekly so that recommendations like diet and exercise can become more personalized.
Does stress directly prove PCOD? +
No. Stress alone does not prove PCOD. In OvaCare, stress is used as a supportive factor for wellness tracking and recommendation logic, not as a standalone medical diagnosis.
Why should I calculate BMI in this project? +
BMI gives a basic idea of body weight status and is one of the useful factors often considered in PCOD-related health tracking. It also helps improve app-side personalization.
How often should I take the stress quiz and PCOD test? +
In OvaCare, the stress quiz is designed for weekly tracking and the PCOD assessment is designed for monthly tracking. This helps you build useful historical trends on your dashboard.
Is OvaCare a replacement for a doctor? +
No. OvaCare is for awareness, early support, self-tracking, and healthy habit guidance only. It does not replace laboratory testing, ultrasound, or medical consultation.
