How Stress Affects PCOD

{% if stress_level == "Low" %}

Your stress level is currently low, which is supportive for hormonal balance. Lower stress helps your body maintain steadier hormone signals and reduces the chance of lifestyle-related triggers making PCOD symptoms worse.

  • Hormonal balance stays more stable.
  • Menstrual cycle regulation is more likely to remain steady.
  • Risk of emotional eating and fatigue is lower.
  • Better mood and energy can support healthier daily habits.
{% elif stress_level == "Moderate" %}

Moderate stress can begin to affect hormones like cortisol, which may indirectly influence insulin balance, appetite, sleep, and cycle regularity. If stress stays elevated for long periods, PCOD symptoms may feel more noticeable.

  • Can increase cortisol and disturb hormone rhythm.
  • May contribute to irregular periods over time.
  • Can trigger cravings or inconsistent eating habits.
  • May worsen tiredness, mood swings, and daily energy.
{% else %}

High stress can strongly affect PCOD because prolonged stress may raise cortisol, disturb insulin response, and interfere with reproductive hormones. This can make symptoms like irregular periods, acne, weight gain, or low energy feel worse.

  • Higher cortisol can disturb hormone balance.
  • Insulin resistance risk may increase further.
  • Symptoms like acne and cycle irregularity may worsen.
  • Stress may affect sleep, mood, appetite, and ovulation patterns.
{% endif %}
